Verum Arisi Adai

Verum Arisi Adai is a simple, traditional South Indian dosa made purely with rice, without any dal (lentils), making it light and crispy.

It is often served with a tangy and spicy Tomato Chutney, creating a perfect balance of flavors for breakfast or a light meal.

Ingredients Required

For the Verum Arisi Adai (Rice Dosa):

  • 2 cups raw rice (any short-grain or parboiled rice works)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water for soaking and grinding
  • Oil or ghee for cooking the dosa

For the Tomato Chutney:

  • 3 ripe tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 1-2 dry red chilies (adjust to spice preference)
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp urad dal (optional)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

    • Steps to Cook

    1. Soak the Rice: Rinse the rice thoroughly in water and soak it for at least 4-5 hours or overnight.
    2. Grind the Batter: Drain the soaked rice and grind it in a blender or wet grinder with enough water to form a smooth, slightly thick batter. The batter should have a pouring consistency, like pancake batter.
    3. Fermentation (Optional): Traditionally, the batter can be fermented for 6-8 hours or overnight for a mild tangy flavor, but this step can be skipped if you prefer instant dosa.
    4. Add Salt: Once the batter is ready, add salt and mix well.
    5. Heat the Griddle: Heat a flat dosa tawa (griddle) over medium heat. Once hot, drizzle a few drops of oil and spread it across the surface with a paper towel or a halved onion to season the tawa.
    6. Spread the Batter: Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the tawa and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin dosa.
    7. Cook the Dosa: Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges. Cook until the edges start to turn golden and crisp. Flip if desired, or serve straight from the tawa once crispy.
    8. Repeat the process for the remaining batter.
    9. Heat Oil: In a pan, heat 1 tablespoon of oil. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and urad dal. Let them splutter.
    10. Add Garlic and Chilies: Add chopped garlic and red chilies, sauté for 30 seconds until fragrant.
    11. Cook Tomatoes and Onion: Add chopped onions and sauté until translucent. Then, add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and break down, about 5-7 minutes.
    12. Season: Add salt and a pinch of asafoetida. Cook until the mixture thickens and the raw smell of tomatoes is gone.
    13. Blend: Allow the mixture to cool slightly, then blend it into a smooth chutney. Adjust seasoning as needed.
    14. Optional Tempering: You can add another tempering of mustard seeds and curry leaves on top of the chutney for extra flavor.
    15. Garnish: Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
    16. Serve the crispy Verum Arisi Adai hot with the tangy Tomato Chutney on the side.
    Enjoy your Verum Arisi Adai!
